*Statik des Fluges* by S. Buchalo is a short work containing a lot of information on the statics and dynamics of flight. In the preface, dated June of 1910, Buchalo writes that it is "only pure chance" that another person did not write this book years ago because the law of conservation of energy and the necessary mechanical principles were known decades prior to the book's publication. Buchalo argues that modern researchers still believe that air pressure is responsible for lifting birds into the air instead of mechanical laws. This force of mass motion, as Buchalo calls it, (or Newton's second law) is 20, 50, or even 100 times greater than air pressure and is the only force that reliably lifts birds into the air.

{{< figure src="wrappers.jpg" title="This copy has the original wrappers and comes from The Library of Congress (duplicate copy)" width="550" class="left">}}

It is Buchalo's goal that "everyone, after reading this work, can form a clear understanding of the forces that arise during flight." He presents his work assuming no special mathematical knowledge, though familiarity with the dynamics of rigid bodies is desirable. He also includes data on flying animals that readers can use to compare their own data with. Some of the topics included in this work are air pressure, a discussion on flight and hovering, the statics and dynamics of flight, measurements in nature (including birds and insects), the conditions required for takeoff, and the flight capability of a flapping-wing aircraft. 

This book contains twelve figures and seven tables. Some of the figures are shown below. The copyright page states that this work is only available from the author, who works at Versuchslaboratorium f&#252;r Flugtechnik (Experimental Laboratory for Flight Technology) in Fellbach. Not much else is known about Buchalo. The *International Catalogue of Scientific Literature: B Mechanics* includes material received between July 1906 and July 1907 and contains an entry for, it is assumed, a paper by Buchalo titled "Einige Luftwiderstandsversuche" ("Some Aerodynamic Drag Tests").
