In the science fiction work *Platonia: A Tale of Other Worlds*, Henry L'Estrange describes a voyage to the moon and then to the planet Platonia. The protagonist Harry is taken on this voyage by his friend Lawrence "Lorry" Raylton, who has built a vehicle capable of traveling through space. The vehicle was "not unlike a torpedo in shape." Not many technical details are given in this book, with Harry stating multiple times that Lorry would eventually write a book with these details.

{{< figure src="title page.jpg" title="Title page of Platonia" width="550" class="left">}}
 
The torpedo is made up of three parts. At the center is the living room. All of the wall space is used up to store "provisions, books, instruments, and the hundreds of oddments necessary for a prolonged journey." The lower third of the torpedo is used to store all the machinery, while the upper third serves as the helm to control the vehicle. A lever is used to accelerate or decelerate. A knob controls the heating of the shell and the lighting of the interior. The metal case can be heated or cooled to adapt to the changing environment during the journey. It is also possible to open large portions of the metal shell to leave a thick layer of glass between the passengers and the outside world in order to steer the ship and also for astronomical observation. The torpedo is propelled by "an enormous storage of electric power, which Lorry could convert at will into mechanical energy." The torpedo is launched by releasing the springs on which the vehicle rests and turning on the motive power at the same time.

Their first destination is the moon, most of which is barren. From Harry's description, "nothing was to be seen but crater, mountain, and arid desert." They are able to view Earth eclipsing the sun. Earth is described as a dark mass that is thirteen times bigger than how the moon appears from Earth. The far side of the moon has clouds, vegetation, and even animal life. On the shores of a vast lake, the two travellers startle a creature that is described as a cross between a pterodactyl and a megalosaurus. It attacks the ship and grabs hold of the springs at the bottom of the ship. At a certain altitude, the springs fall off from the weight of the creature, and the creature tumbles back towards the surface of the moon.

The travelers end up getting caught in a meteor storm and find themselves on the planet of Platonia, where their vehicle becomes suspended above a hill. Much of the novel takes place on Platonia, which is a utopian society and where, starting at about 100,000 miles above the surface, the velocity of light is diminished and eventually reaches two inches per second. This allows the Platonians to see events on Earth from 1780. The book describes the customs of the Platonians and the capital city of Campanella. The two adventurers eventually fall in love with two Platonian women. The book ends with a catastrophic event. An enormous comet throws Platonia out of orbit and destroys all life on the planet. However, Harry and Lorry are able to use the torpedo and escape with their wives back to Earth.

This copy is in its original wrappers. The rear wrapper bears the seal of W. H. Smith & Son of London, which began as a newspaper vendor in 1792.
