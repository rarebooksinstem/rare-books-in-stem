This work is the first English-language translation of Jean Mocquet's *Voyages en Afrique, Asie, Indes orientales et occidentales*, first printed in 1617, with further editions in 1645 and 1665. A Dutch translation appeared in 1656, though it was "very inaccurate" (*M&#233;moires de la Soci&#233;t&#233; nationale acad&#233;mique de Cherbourg*, printed in 1904-1905). Mocquet was born near the town of Vienne in Dauphin&#233;, a former province in southeastern France. He was encouraged by Henry IV to travel to foreign countries and write about his experiences. His travels were also supported by Louis XIII, who set up a room at the Tuileries Garden where Mocquet could put all the curiosities and rarities he had acquired during his travels. Mocquet, who was already the King's apothecary, was made curator of this "royal museum" (*M&#233;moires de la Soci&#233;t&#233; nationale acad&#233;mique de Cherbourg* and *Les sources in&#233;dites de l'histoire du Maroc de 1530 &#224; 1845*). Given that the final mention of Mocquet is in his book from 1617, it is presumed that he died that year. 

This work is divided into six "books," each dealing with a different part of the world. The first four books contain eight full-length pages of woodcuts for a total of 16 woodcuts. These are shown below. In the preface, Mocquet discusses principles of the astronomy, cosmography, and geography. The first book describes Mocquet's travels to "Libia, the Canaries, and Barbary." The second book contains his account of the West Indies and "other Countries of the West." The third talks about his travels to Morocco and other places in Africa. The fourth book describes places in Africa and the East Indies, including Mozambique and Goa. The fifth book describes his travels to Syria, Jerusalem, and "the Holy-Land." The sixth and final book details his time in Spain working as an apothecary and how misfortunes kept him from accomplishing his goal of traveling around the world ("encompass the whole Universe") as famous explorers before him had done.

This copy contains the bookplates of Neva and Guy Littell and Herman le Roy Edgar. The Littells were book collectors based in Kenilworth, Illinois. Many of the books in their collection can be found in various catalogs. *The Library of Herman Le Roy Edgar (Part Two)*, printed in 1921 by The Anderson Galleries contains an entry for this copy, noting that it comes in "half wine-color morocco, gilt top, [bound] by Stikeman."
