This scarce edition of Euclid's *Elements* was printed in 1770 and edited by Joseph Fenn. The title pages of this work and other works by Fenn list him as a Professor of Philosophy at the University of Nants (Nantes). It appears that this work was part of Fenn's *Instructions Given in the Drawing School*, which was a series of lectures intended "to enable Youth to become Proficients in the different Branches of that Art, and to pursue with Success, Geographical, Nautical, Mechanical, Commerical, and Military Studies." What is interesting about this particular edition is that it only contains 12 of the 13 books typically found in Euclid's *Elements*. The final book discussing the five Platonic solids (tetrahedron, cube, octahedron, dodecahedron, and icosahedron) is not included in this edition, and it is unclear why it was omitted.

{{< figure src="title page.jpg" title="Title page of Euclid's Elements, edited by Joseph Fenn" width="550" class="left">}}

There are many woodcut diagrams to accompany the text, including illustrations at the end of each book or section. This book was printed by Alexander McCulloch, "an extensive Dublin bookseller" who also published the "Dublin Evening Post" (*The History of Irish Periodical Literature* by Richard Robert Madden). This copy is bound in contemporary calf with a paper label on the spine.
