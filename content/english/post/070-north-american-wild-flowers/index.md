*North American Wild Flowers* is the result of ten years of work by Mary Vaux Walcott. She, along with her husband Dr. Charles Doolittle Walcott (a paleontologist and the fourth Secretary of the Smithsonian), spent three to four months each year in the Canadian Rockies finding and painting wild flowers. The 400 plates found across the five volumes are based on drawings by Walcott. According to the "Bulletin of the Brooklyn Public Library" from April of 1928, Walcott's paintings "have been reproduced by a refined photographic process, using as many colors and printings as were necessary to obtain accurate and artistic reproductions. The method of printing the plates, however, was unique; for they were printed by a special process, on an all-rag paper, thus insuring an artistic appearance as well as permanence. The specially-made paper and the method of utilization give the prints the appearance of paintings; and it is difficult, at a short distance, to distinguish between the original and the reproduction." Each plate is accompanied by one or two pages describing the flower's habitat, soil, elevation, and range.

This five-volume set originally came unbound, and it was up to the buyer to get it bound to their liking. This trade edition was preceded by a limited edition of 500 copies signed by the author and was available by subscription for a price of $500. This particular copy bears the bookplate of Laura D. Babson, who was presumably the first owner of this copy and had it bound in green morocco.
