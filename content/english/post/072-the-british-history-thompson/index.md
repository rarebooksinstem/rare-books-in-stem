The 1718 printing of *The British History, Translated into English from the Latin of Jeffrey of Monmouth* is the earliest edition of Geoffrey of Monmouth's *De gestis Britonum or Historia Regum Britanniae*. This edition was translated by Aaron Thompson (1681 or 1682 - 1751), who, in the lengthy preface, defends the credibility of Geoffrey, though his was "most definitely an outlier opinion" (*A Companion to Geoffrey of Monmouth*). Thompson places the date of publication of *De gestis Britonum or Historia Regum Britanniae* between 1123 and 1128.

{{< figure src="title page.jpg" title="Title page" width="550" class="left">}}

After the preface is a list of subscribers, and among them is Sir Isaac Newton. This work is divided into 12 "books," of which the ninth, tenth, and a portion of the eleventh talk about King Arthur, including the legend of Merlin. This 1718 translation was reprinted in 1848 in *Six Old English Chronicles*, edited by John Allen Giles.
